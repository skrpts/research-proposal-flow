## Purpose

Reference document for research ethics requirements. Used when designing methodology and preparing ethics applications.

## Core Principles

### Respect for Persons
- Informed consent: participants must understand what they are agreeing to
- Voluntary participation: no coercion or undue influence
- Right to withdraw at any time without consequence
- Privacy and confidentiality of personal data

### Beneficence
- Research should aim to produce benefit
- Risks must be minimised and proportionate to expected benefits
- Researchers must be competent to conduct the proposed research

### Justice
- Fair selection of participants — not targeting vulnerable groups for convenience
- Benefits and burdens of research distributed equitably
- Vulnerable populations given additional protections

### Integrity
- Honest reporting of methods and findings
- No fabrication, falsification, or plagiarism
- Conflicts of interest disclosed
- Data managed responsibly and transparently

## Key Requirements

### Before Data Collection
- Ethics committee/IRB approval obtained
- Informed consent materials prepared and approved
- Data management plan in place
- Risk assessment completed

### During Data Collection
- Ongoing consent maintained
- Adverse events reported and managed
- Data stored securely
- Participant welfare monitored

### After Data Collection
- Data anonymised or pseudonymised as appropriate
- Results reported accurately and completely
- Negative results reported (no publication bias)
- Data retained per institutional and funder requirements
- Participants informed of findings if agreed

## Data Protection

- Comply with applicable data protection legislation (e.g., UK GDPR, DPA 2018)
- Data minimisation: collect only what is needed
- Purpose limitation: use data only for stated purposes
- Storage limitation: retain only as long as necessary
- Lawful basis for processing identified and documented
